About Troop 1941

Service, leadership, and adventure rooted in Ashburn.

Troop 1941 is a Scouts BSA troop chartered in Ashburn, Virginia. We meet each week at Farmwell Station Middle School to plan campouts, hone outdoor skills, and strengthen our patrols. Families are welcome to visit and see how we work together.

Program highlights

  • Monthly campouts, hikes, and high-adventure trips.
  • Leadership training and advancement support for every rank.
  • Service projects that give back to the Ashburn community.

Who can join

  • Scouts BSA youth ages 11-17 ready for teamwork.
  • Families who value leadership through the outdoors.
  • New scouts are always welcome to visit a meeting.

What to expect

Meetings built for active scouting.

A typical Troop 1941 meeting starts with opening flags, moves into patrol time and skills instruction, and closes with announcements about upcoming adventures. Guests can talk with our Scoutmaster team, while scouts jump into hands-on activities.

✓
Hands-on learning. Lashings, first aid, knots, map & compass, and more.
✓
Patrol teamwork. Scouts plan menus, gear, and campout logistics together.
✓
Leadership growth. Positions of responsibility and coaching for every rank.
